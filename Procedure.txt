  <div class="main">
  <h3><b><u>Procedure for Hot Mounting : </b></u></h3>
  <p><ol style="1">
        <li>	The specimen should be cleaned before mounting in order to improve adhesion to the mounting medium. Use acetone or alcohol to clean it. Then dry the specimen well.</li><br> 
        <li>Switch on the hot press mount.</li><br>
        <li>Apply anti-stick stearate powder to upper and lower rams of the cylinder to avoid sticking of resin.</li><br>
        <li>Place the specimen on the lower ram with the surface to be viewed facing upwards.</li><br>
        <li>Select the most suitable hot-mounting resin depending on your requirements.</li><br>
        <li>Adjust the movable lower ram to the lowest position and fill the cylinder with ~1 scoop of resin.</li><br>
        <li>Now place the closure with the upper ram onto the mounting cylinder and turn it to lock it in place.</li><br>
        <li>Set the hot mounting parameters (temperature, pressure, cooling) to the appropriate settings depending on resin used.</li><br>
        <li>Press start and wait; the machine will automatically relieve pressure when the cooling time has elapsed.</li><br>
        <li>Turn the top closure to unlock it and raise the lower ram to the upper limit.</li><br>
        <li>Remove the sample from the cylinder and switch off the hot press mount.</li></ol>
  </p>
  <br>
  <h3><b><u>Procedure for Cold Mounting : </b></u></h3>
  <p><ol style="1">
        <li>Adjust the vacuum to maximum and pump down the chamber.</li><br>
        <li>Place the tubing on the metal stand and tighten the knob.</li><br>
        <li>Tighten stopper and knob to pump down to reach about 400m bar.</li><br>
        <li>Allow the vacuum to hold for about 5 min.</li><br>
        <li>Preparation of pouring epoxy over the specimen.</li><br>
        <li>Prepare epoxy compound with 20ml of epoxy resin and 5ml of hardener, stir slowly for 6min reduce the formation of bubbles.</li><br>
        <li>Place the tubing into epoxy compound and release the knob to allow flowing (try to not release any bubbles to the mould).</li><br>
        <li>After the addition of sufficient epoxy, tighten the knob to stop the flow of epoxy.</li><br>
        <li>Adjust the vacuum to minimum and wait for 30 minutes.</li><br>
        <li>Obtain the mould.</li><br>
        <li>Vent the chamber and remove the mould with the sample inside and leave it to harden for 8 hours before removing the cold mount for mould.</li></ol>
  </p>
